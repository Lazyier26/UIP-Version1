Do you have questions?

Below, you will find answers to frequently asked questions related to onboarding, offboarding, and navigating your experience as a new intern.

New Intern
Where can I get my application ID?
The application ID will be provided once you've registered for the pre-orientation attendance form.
Where should I ask if I have more concerns?
If you have additional concerns or questions, you can reach out to our onboarding team through either Discord or email. For Discord, please use the Newbies channel to connect with the team.
What should I do if I want to withdraw from my internship at your company?
Simply send us an email along with your withdrawal letter.
There's no MOA between the company and our school. What should I do?
You can request a Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) from your school, and we're more than willing to sign it.
Onboarding
Can interns start with their internship without undergoing company orientation?
No, we highly recommend attending the preboarding orientation to get acquainted with our procedures and FAQs.
Who is the contact person that I should put on my required documents?
The contact person you should put is Mrs. Jonalyn Estrada, our UIP Director.
What should I put in the remaining hours of my daily journal #1?
Deduct the hours used on your first day from your total hours, and that's what you'll record in the remaining hours on your daily journal #1.
I want to ask for an advance Certificate of Completion (COC). Is it possible to request it?
To ensure fairness, please complete your internship hours at UIP before requesting an advance COC.
Offboarding
Who do I contact for the offboarding process?
The contact person is Mr. Deanver Lucena. You may ping him on the offboarding channel in UIP's discord server.
How do I know if I went to offboard?
After you completed OJT hours, you may check it on your tracker account.

ONBOARDING PROCESS

Step 1

VISIT THE WEBSITE AND REGISTER

Click on the JOIN US button on our website.

Completely fill up the application form with complete and accurate details.

Step 2

CHECK EMAIL INBOX FOR CONFIRMATION EMAIL

Monitor your email as our team will be sending a confirmation email.

This will include the link to access our pre-boarding orientation.

Step 3

ATTEND THE PRE-BOARDING ORIENTATION

We highly recommend your attendance at the pre-boarding orientation.

This will provide important information and instructions to start your internship.

Step 4

DEPLOYMENT AND ATTENDANCE TRACKER

You will be assigned to one of our esteemed partnered companies.

Register on the attendance tracker. It can be accessed through https://www.uip.ph/attendance.

Step 5

JOIN UIP AND COMPANY DISCORD SERVERS

Join both the UIP and your assigned company's Discord server. The UIP's main discord can be found here: https://discord.gg/NcRqGFWeQd

It will serve as the primary communication platform with your team.

OFFBOARDING PROCESS

Step 1

VISIT THE WEBSITE AND REGISTER

Register at https://uip.ph/exit/

Make sure that all the requirements are accomplished accordingly.

Step 2

CHECK EMAIL INBOX FOR REGISTRATION STATUS

Once your offboarding registration is approved, you'll receive an email that contains the file name of your COC template.

You can now choose whether you want to request a hard copy or a scanned copy of your COC.

Step 3

REQUEST FOR A HARD COPY OF YOUR COC

If you wish to receive a hard copy of your COC, kindly place an order on Shopee using the following link: https://shopee.ph/product /990492443/18879237781/

Step 4

REQUEST FOR A SCANNED COPY OF YOUR COC

If you prefer a scanned copy of your COC, please complete the request form at the following link: https://forms.gle/uConWScrGJum2Jbp7.

Fill out all required information, and we will process your request. The scanned copy will be sent to the email address provided.

Step 5

DELIVERY / SENDING OF COC

Please wait patiently for the release of your COC. A physical copy will be delivered to you, while those who requested for a scanned copy will receive it via email.

Ensure that all issued equipment and materials are returned to the company. This includes any borrowed tools, devices, and documents. Failure to do so may result in charges or delays in your clearance process.
